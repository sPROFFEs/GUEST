What hosts on {{ lan_iface }} ({{ lan_cidr }}) can reach beyond the gateway.

How this works

By default the gateway NATs everything from the internal LAN out through {{ wan_iface }}. That includes any private LAN reachable through the WAN side. Use this page to seal that off:

  1. Restricted subnets below are blocked for traffic originating in the internal LAN.
  2. Allowlist rules punch holes through that block — they're evaluated before the drop.
  3. Anything not matching either list (i.e. the public internet) is allowed, just like before.

Empty restricted-subnets list = no restriction at all (compatible with setups where you don't need to seal off any private network). Changes need Apply to take effect.
